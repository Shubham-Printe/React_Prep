# Final Round — HR & Behavioural Prep (Atlas Copco)

Use this for the **HR/behavioural** part of the final round: experience, motivation, teamwork, and fit.

---

## 1. STAR format (use for any “tell me about a time…” question)

- **S**ituation — Brief context (project, team, constraint).
- **T**ask — Your responsibility or goal.
- **A**ction — What you did (steps, decisions, collaboration).
- **R**esult — Outcome (impact, metric, or lesson).

Keep answers to **2–3 minutes** unless they ask for more detail. End with a clear result.

### Your IoT angle (final year project — use when showing willingness/familiarity)

**What it was:** Final year project (~7 years ago): use **traffic data from a public API** to **dynamically adjust traffic light timings**. Problem in mind: two lanes with unnecessary green while others are packed — frustrating and often needs traffic police to step in. We used an **Arduino with a SIM module** to fetch the traffic data.

**A bit more (for delivery):** The SIM module gave the Arduino **cellular connectivity** so it could call the API from the field without being wired to a PC. The idea was: get **live traffic data** (e.g. congestion or volume per approach), run **simple logic** to decide how long each signal should stay green, and **reduce wasted green time** on empty lanes while giving more time to congested ones. So it was **data from the real world driving control decisions** — similar in spirit to industrial setups where sensors or devices feed data and the system responds. You had exposure to **device + connectivity + logic**, not just theory.

**When to use it:** When they ask about interest in IoT/embedded, or “any experience with devices/connectivity,” or when you want to show you’re not new to the idea of hardware + data + logic.

**Short version (1–2 sentences):** “In my final year I did an IoT project — we used traffic data from a public API to dynamically adjust traffic light timings, with an Arduino and SIM module fetching the data. It’s been a while, but it’s where I got a taste of device connectivity and real-world data driving decisions. I’d like to revisit that kind of setup in a role like this.”

**Richer version (when you have 30–40 seconds):** “In my final year I did a project on **smart traffic signals** — we used an **Arduino with a SIM module** to fetch live traffic data from a public API and then **dynamically adjust signal timings** so we didn’t waste green on empty lanes while others were packed. So it was **data from the field driving control logic** — device, connectivity, and a simple decision loop. It’s been a few years, but that’s where I got familiar with the idea of devices talking to APIs and acting on real-world data. I’d like to build on that in a role like this.”

**Don’t:** Overclaim (“I’m an IoT expert”). Do: “Familiarity from that project; keen to build on it here.”

### “Describe a conflict with a teammate / stakeholder. How did you resolve it?”

Use **STAR**. Keep it professional; focus on resolution, not blame.

- **S/T:** Disagreement on scope, timeline, or approach (e.g. “a stakeholder wanted more features before demo; we had a fixed deadline”).
- **A:** Listened to their concerns, laid out constraints (time, quality), proposed a phased plan (MVP now, rest later), got alignment in writing.
- **R:** Shipped on time; relationship stayed good; later phases delivered as planned.

---

### “Describe a time you failed or missed a deadline.”

- **S/T:** Brief context (e.g. underestimated integration effort or a dependency slipped).
- **A:** What you did: communicated early, re-scoped or got help, documented what went wrong.
- **R:** What you learned: better estimation, earlier risk flagging, or clearer acceptance criteria. One concrete change you made after.

## 3. Questions you should ask (HR/behavioural part)

**Role & team**

- What does success look like in the first 30/60/90 days?
- Who would I work with day-to-day (team, manager, stakeholders)?
- How does the web/UI team work with embedded or backend engineers?

**Work setup**

- What’s the work model (on-site / hybrid / remote) and typical week?
- What’s the notice period you need, and is there flexibility?

**Growth & culture**

- How does the team balance new features vs tech debt and stability?
- Are there learning budgets or support for certifications?

**Process**

- What are the next steps and timeline after this round?
- When can I expect to hear back?

Pick **3–4** that feel most important to you and write them down before the call.

---

## 4. Red flags to watch (without being negative)

- Very vague role (“we’ll figure out the team later”).
- No clarity on compensation structure (fixed vs variable, when you get it in writing).
- Pressure to accept verbally before a written offer.
- Big mismatch between what was discussed in technical rounds and what HR describes (level, scope, location).

If any of these show up, ask **one clear follow-up** (e.g. “Can we get the role and team clarified before I make a decision?” or “When can I expect the written offer with the full CTC breakup?”).

---

## 5. Closing the HR part

- Thank them for the time and the clarity.
- Repeat your interest: “I’m very interested in the role and the team. I’d be happy to move forward once we align on the details we discussed.”
- If compensation was discussed: “I’ll need the written offer with the full breakup to confirm. When can I expect that?”
- Ask: “What are the next steps and by when should I expect to hear from you?”
