# Salary Negotiation Simulation — All Scenarios

You’ve cleared technical and HR. This is the **salary discussion**. Use this to practice with a friend (they read Employer) or out loud by yourself.

**Your numbers:** Current 13 LPA | Expectation 20–22 LPA | Min 18 LPA

---

## Scenario 1 — BEST CASE

**What it is:** They have budget, they want to close. Offer at or above your target (20–22), smooth conversation.

### Dialogue

**Employer:** “Shubham, we’re really happy with how the rounds went. We’d like to move forward. You’d mentioned your current CTC is 13 LPA and you’re looking at a 50–60% raise. We’re looking at **22 LPA fixed** as part of the offer. There’s a variable component of about 10% on top, paid annually based on performance. Does that work for you?”

**You (suggested):**
- Thank them. Confirm you’re interested.
- Ask for the full written breakup before confirming: “That sounds in line with what I was looking for. I’d like to see the **full written offer** — fixed, variable, allowances, and any other components — so I can confirm and revert by [date]. When can I expect that?”

**Employer:** “Sure. You’ll get the written offer by [e.g. Friday] with the full CTC breakup. We’ll also send the benefits summary — insurance, leave, WFH policy. Any other questions?”

**You (suggested):**
- One clarifier: “Could you confirm when the variable is paid and what the performance criteria are?”
- Then: “I don’t have anything else on compensation. Thank you — I’ll review the offer and get back to you.”

---

## Scenario 2 — GOOD CASE

**What it is:** They offer 20 LPA fixed (low end of your range). No pushback, but you want to try for 22 or a sign-on.

### Dialogue

**Employer:** “We’d like to extend an offer. We’re looking at **20 LPA fixed**, plus variable of around 8–10% based on annual performance. Given your experience and what you shared, we feel this is a strong offer. What do you think?”

**You (suggested):**
- Don’t say yes immediately. Don’t say no.
- “Thanks for sharing the number. I had shared my expectation of 20–22 LPA — 20 is at the lower end of that range. Is there any room to take the fixed to **22 LPA**, or to bridge the gap with a **sign-on bonus**? That would help me say yes with confidence.”

**Employer (friendly):** “I’ll need to check with the hiring manager. We might be able to do a one-time sign-on to bridge the gap — say, X months of the difference. I can’t promise 22 fixed, but I’ll come back with our best number and the written breakup. Would that work?”

**You (suggested):**
- “Yes, that works. I’d need the full written breakup — fixed, variable, and sign-on if applicable — and the payout terms. Once I have that, I’ll review and revert.”

---

## Scenario 3 — ACCEPTABLE

**What it is:** They offer 20 LPA fixed, and they clearly can’t move (or only marginally). You’re okay with 20 if the rest is clear.

### Dialogue

**Employer:** “We’re pleased to offer **20 LPA fixed**. Variable is 10%, paid annually. We’ve stretched a bit to get here given internal bands. I don’t think we can go higher on fixed, but the role and growth here are strong. Can you work with this?”

**You (suggested):**
- If 20 is acceptable to you: “I was hoping for the upper end of my range, but I understand. I can work with **20 LPA fixed** provided I get the **full written offer** with the breakup — fixed, variable, criteria for variable, and benefits. Once I have that, I’ll confirm.”

**Employer:** “You’ll get the written offer by [date]. Anything else?”

**You (suggested):**
- “Just the clarifiers we discussed — variable payout timing and criteria. Otherwise I’m good. Thank you.”

---

## Scenario 4 — NEED TO PUSH

**What it is:** First number is below your range (e.g. 17–18 LPA). You need to push back clearly but professionally.

### Dialogue

**Employer:** “We’d like to offer **17 LPA fixed**, with variable of about 15%. Our bands for this level typically cap around here. We see good potential in you. What are your thoughts?”

**You (suggested):**
- Don’t accept. Don’t be aggressive.
- “I appreciate you sharing the number. I had shared that I’m at 13 LPA and looking at a 50–60% raise, which puts me in the **20–22 LPA** range for fixed. **17 is below what I can accept** given my experience and the move. Is there any room to revisit — either on fixed or with a sign-on — so we can get closer to that range?”

**Employer (defensive):** “Our bands are quite fixed. I’m not sure we can go to 20. Maybe we can do 18 and a small sign-on?”

**You (suggested):**
- “18 fixed plus a sign-on could work if the **total** brings me meaningfully closer to my expectation and the sign-on terms are clear in writing. I’d need the full breakup and the clawback period for the sign-on. Can you share what’s the maximum you can do on fixed + sign-on combined?”

**Employer:** “Let me check and get back with a revised number and the written breakup.”

**You (suggested):**
- “That works. I’ll wait for the written offer. Thank you.”

---

## Scenario 5 — POLITELY DECLINE

**What it is:** Their best offer stays below your minimum (e.g. 18 or below and they won’t move). You decline without burning bridges.

### Dialogue

**Employer:** “We’ve done what we can. Our best is **18 LPA fixed** and 10% variable. We can’t go beyond this for the role. We’d really like to have you. Can you accept?”

**You (suggested):**
- Be clear, not emotional. No “you’re wrong” or long justification.
- “I really appreciate the time and the offer. Given my situation and what I’m looking for, **I’m not able to accept at this number**. I hope you understand. I’ve enjoyed learning about Atlas Copco and the team, and I’d be open to staying in touch if things change on either side. Thank you again.”

**Employer:** “We’re sorry to hear that. If your expectations change, do reach out. All the best.”

**You (suggested):**
- “Thank you. All the best to the team too.”

## Quick reference — Your lines by scenario

| Scenario      | Your move |
|---------------|-----------|
| **Best**      | Thank them; ask for written offer + variable clarity; confirm interest. |
| **Good**      | “20 is at the lower end; room for 22 or sign-on?” → Get best number + written breakup. |
| **Acceptable**| “I can work with 20 if I get full written breakup and variable criteria.” |
| **Push**      | “17/18 is below what I can accept. Room to revisit fixed or sign-on?” Hold for 20 or walk. |
| **Decline**   | “I’m not able to accept at this number. Thank you; open to staying in touch.” |

---

## Practice tips

- Say your lines **out loud** at least once per scenario.
- Time yourself: keep each response to **30–60 seconds**.
- In real life, **pause** before answering; it’s fine to say “Let me think for a moment.”
