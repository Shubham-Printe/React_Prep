# Offer at 19.5 LPA + 5% variable — Quick negotiation

**Offer:** 19.5 LPA CTC (5% variable on basic included in this)  
**Context:** Confirm ASAP — hiring freeze coming.  
**Your anchor:** 20–22 LPA (floor ~20 LPA effective).

---

## 1. Where you stand

- **19.5 LPA is total CTC** — the 5% variable on basic is **included** in that 19.5, not on top. So effective = **19.5 LPA**. You’re **0.5 L below** your floor of 20 LPA.
- **Hiring freeze** = they want a fast yes, but it also means **one shot** to ask. No long back-and-forth.

---

## 2. One-shot ask (don’t drag)

**Goal:** Try once for **20 LPA fixed** OR **joining bonus (0.5–1 L)** to bridge. Then either align or accept as-is.

**On call (if they ring to discuss):**

- “Thank you for the offer. I’d shared my expectation of 20–22 LPA. The offer is at 19.5 LPA total (with 5% variable included), which is slightly below my floor. Could we take it to **20 LPA** (CTC), or add a **one-time joining bonus** of around **₹50,000–1,00,000** to bridge the gap? I’m ready to confirm as soon as we align.”

**By email (if you’re replying in writing):**

- “Thank you for the offer. I’d shared my expectation of 20–22 LPA. The offer at 19.5 LPA (with 5% variable on basic included) is slightly below that. Would it be possible to move to **20 LPA** CTC, or to add a **one-time joining bonus** of **₹50,000–1,00,000**? I am ready to confirm once we align, and I understand the need to close quickly.”

Then **stop**. No long justification.

---

## 3. If they say no (“no room,” “freeze,” “this is final”)

- **Option A — Accept:** 19.5 LPA is 0.5 L below your floor. If the role, company, and growth matter more than the gap, accept and confirm.
- **Option B — Walk:** Only if you have a strong alternative or 19.5 is below your walk-away number.

Don’t push again after a clear “no.” Either accept or decline.

---

## 4. Before you sign

- [ ] **Written breakup:** Fixed, variable (5% on basic), HRA, allowances, PF, any other components. Confirm basic so you know exact variable amount.
- [ ] **Variable:** Payout timing (annual/half-yearly?), criteria (individual/company), and that it’s mentioned in the offer letter.
- [ ] **Joining bonus:** If they agree, get amount and payout (joining/first payroll) and any clawback in writing.

## The script

**Why this order:** (1) Thank you + excitement first so you’re a “yes” candidate, not a threat. (2) **Reasoning before the number** — they hear *why* it matters before they hear 21, so the number lands as reasonable, not demanding. (3) One clear ask at the end (22 → close at 21), then pause. (4) Fallback (sign-on) only *after* they say no — don’t offer them an easy out upfront.

---

### 1. Opening (rapport + signal you want to say yes)

“Hi, first of all — thank you for the offer. I’m very excited to join Atlas Copco. I’ve seen the work environment, and I’m drawn towards the opportunities this position will give me. I’m trying to find a way to make this work — and that brings me to compensation. I have an ask, and I’d like to give you a bit of context first so it makes sense.”

*[She may say “Sure, go ahead” or “What is it?” or simply wait. Either way, you continue.]*

---

### 2. Reasoning first (so they’re primed before they hear the number)

“So — two things. First, my appraisal is next week, and my CTC will go to at least 15 LPA. So from that point, 21 isn’t a huge jump; it’s a reasonable step for this move.

Second — from what I understand, Atlas isn’t known for strong increments or fast promotions. So for me, the joining number matters a lot. I’d feel much more confident about the move if we could get there.”

*[Brief pause. She may nod or say something. Then you give the number.]*

---

### 3. The ask (anchor → concession → one clear question)

“I’d mentioned 20–22 when we spoke. I was thinking **22** — but I’m happy to **close at 21 fixed.** Is there any way we can get there?”

*[Pause. Say nothing. Let her respond.]*

---

### 4. Only if she says no or “we can’t do 21” (fallback — don’t say this until she’s said no)

“I understand. Can we then look at the best fixed you can offer and bridge the gap with a **one-time sign-on**? For example, **20 LPA fixed plus a sign-on of ₹1 lakh** — so I’m closer to that and we can close with confidence. I’m really trying to find a way to make this work.”

*[Pause.]*

---

### 5. If she says she’ll check / revert

“Sure. I’d really appreciate it if you could check and revert. I’m ready to confirm as soon as we align — and again, I’m very excited to join. I understand the hiring freeze and the need to close quickly.”
