## HR Connect — Pointers to Keep in Mind

### Goals for the call
- **Get clarity**: role scope + level/title + team + expectations.
- **Lock logistics**: location/remote, start date, notice period, work authorization.
- **Close the loop**: compensation + benefits + offer process/timeline.

### Role & level alignment
- **Title/Level**: confirm the official level/title and what that maps to internally.
- **Team & manager**: which team, who you report to, hiring manager’s name.
- **Scope**: day-to-day responsibilities, ownership, on-call/support expectations (if any).
- **Success criteria**: what “good in 30/60/90 days” looks like.
  - **Why this matters**: aligns expectations early and avoids surprises in probation/reviews.
  - **What to clarify**:
    - **30 days**: onboarding + understanding product/codebase + first small wins.
    - **60 days**: independent delivery + ownership of a module/feature area.
    - **90 days**: measurable impact + reliability/quality improvements + stakeholder trust.
  - **Ask HR**: “How do you define success for this role in the first 30/60/90 days, and how will it be evaluated?”
- **Work model**: hybrid — **2 days/week in office** (Kharadi, Pune). Confirm how strict this is, whether days are fixed/flexible, and clarify any travel expectations.

### Compensation (ask for the full breakdown)
- **Base salary**: range + final target.
  - **Meaning**: fixed monthly component (the most reliable part of compensation).
  - **Ask HR**: “What is the base salary range for this role and what base are you proposing for me? Please share it in the written CTC breakup.”
- **Bonus**: target %, payout schedule, performance criteria.
  - **Meaning**: variable pay; confirm if it’s guaranteed or performance-dependent.
  - **Ask HR**: “What is the target bonus %, how is it calculated, and when is it paid? Is it prorated in year 1?”
- **Equity** (if applicable): amount, vesting schedule, cliff, refreshers.
  - **Meaning**: ownership component (ESOP/RSU); value depends on company terms.
  - **Ask HR**: “Is there equity/ESOP? What is the grant size, vesting schedule, cliff, and are refreshers provided?”
- **Sign-on**: amount, payout timing, clawback terms.
  - **Meaning**: one-time payment; usually has repayment/clawback conditions.
  - **Ask HR**: “Is there a sign-on bonus? When is it paid (joining/after X months), and what are the clawback terms?”
- **CTC structure**: fixed vs variable, allowances, deductions (important in India).
  - **Meaning**: the complete salary structure; CTC ≠ in-hand.
  - **Ask HR**: “Can you share the full CTC breakup with fixed vs variable, allowances, employer PF/insurance, and expected in-hand?”
- **Revision cycle**: when the first appraisal/review happens.
  - **Meaning**: timing of your first performance review and potential pay adjustment.
  - **Ask HR**: “When is the next appraisal cycle, and when would I be eligible for my first salary revision?”

### Benefits & policies (quick but important)
- **Health insurance**: coverage for self/spouse/parents, premium splits, waiting period.
  - **Meaning**: the practical medical coverage you and family actually get from day 1 (and what you pay for it).
  - **Ask HR**: “Can you share health insurance details: sum insured, coverage for spouse/parents, waiting period, and employee contribution?”
- **PTO/holidays**: annual leaves, sick leave, carry-forward/encashment.
  - **Meaning**: time-off flexibility + whether unused leaves carry forward or get encashed.
  - **Ask HR**: “What is the annual PTO + sick leave policy, and what are the rules for carry-forward/encashment?”
- **Work hours**: flexibility, core hours, overtime/comp-off policy.
  - **Meaning**: real working expectations (hours, core overlap) and how extra hours are handled.
  - **Ask HR**: “What are typical working hours and core hours? Is there any overtime/comp-off policy?”
- **Equipment**: laptop, WFH allowance, internet reimbursement.
  - **Meaning**: what the company provides vs what you’re expected to self-fund.
  - **Ask HR**: “Do you provide laptop and accessories? Any WFH allowance or internet reimbursement?”
- **Learning budget**: courses/certifications/conferences.
  - **Meaning**: how much support you get for upskilling and whether it’s easy to use.
  - **Ask HR**: “Is there an annual learning budget for courses/certs/conferences? What’s the approval process?”
- **Notice period**: theirs and yours, buyout options.
  - **Meaning**: exit/joining flexibility; buyout rules can impact your joining plan.
  - **Ask HR**: “What’s the notice period policy for this role, and is buyout/early release supported?”
- **Background verification**: what documents they need and when.
  - **Meaning**: document readiness + timeline risk; know what’s required early.
  - **Ask HR**: “What checks are part of BGV, what documents do you need, and when do you start the process?”

### Offer process & next steps
- **Timeline**: when to expect the written offer.
  - **Meaning**: sets expectations; helps you plan notice period and other processes.
  - **Ask HR**: “When can I expect the written offer, and by what date do you expect a decision?”
- **Rounds left**: any additional checks (VP, bar-raiser, reference calls).
  - **Meaning**: confirms if hiring is truly complete or if anything is pending.
  - **Ask HR**: “Is anything still pending—reference checks, final approvals, or any additional round?”
- **Documentation**: payslips, offer letters, ID, education, etc.
  - **Meaning**: reduces delays; ensures you can submit everything quickly.
  - **Ask HR**: “Please list the documents you need for offer/BGV (payslips, offer letters, ID, education, etc.) and the deadline for each.”
- **Joining constraints**: your notice period and preferred joining window.
  - **Meaning**: aligns start date expectations and avoids last-minute friction.
  - **Ask HR**: “Given my notice period, what joining window works for you? Is the joining date flexible if my release date shifts?”

### Negotiation hygiene (keep it clean)
- **Ask for written details** before committing verbally.
  - **Meaning**: prevents misunderstandings; ensures the final offer matches what was discussed.
  - **Ask HR**: “Could you please share the complete offer/CTC breakup and benefits in writing so I can review and confirm?”
- **Share expectations as a range** (not a single number), and anchor to the **complete package** (base + bonus + equity + sign-on).
  - **Meaning**: you negotiate on total value, not just one component.
  - **Ask HR**: “What’s the budgeted range for this role? I’d like to align on the full package (base + variable + any equity + sign-on).”
- **Clarify constraints**: notice period, competing timelines (if any), flexibility on joining.
  - **Meaning**: avoids timeline pressure and keeps the process smooth.
  - **Ask HR**: “What’s the expected decision timeline and offer validity? How flexible is the joining date based on notice period?”

### Red flags to watch for
- **Vague role/team** (“we’ll decide later”).
  - **Meaning**: scope drift risk; you might land in a different team than expected.
  - **How to probe**: “Can we confirm the exact team, reporting manager, and role scope in the offer/role description?”
- **Unclear variable pay rules** or large variable without clarity.
  - **Meaning**: pay uncertainty; variable may not pay out as expected.
  - **How to probe**: “Can you share the variable pay formula, past payout trends, and what percentage is typically paid out?”
- **Pressure to accept quickly** without written offer.
  - **Meaning**: decision risk without full information.
  - **How to probe**: “What is the offer validity period once I receive the written offer? Can I have X days to review?”
- **Mismatch** between interview scope and HR-stated expectations/level.
  - **Meaning**: misalignment on seniority, responsibilities, or compensation band.
  - **How to probe**: “Can we confirm the level/title and responsibilities match what was discussed with the Engineering Manager?”

### Offer acceptance checklist (must confirm in writing)

- **Role/Title**
  - **Must have**: Senior Frontend Engineer (or equivalent) clearly stated on offer letter
  - **Acceptable**: matches what was discussed in interviews; no surprise downgrade in title/level

- **Team & reporting**
  - **Must have**: reporting manager name + team/org
  - **Acceptable**: known team/manager at offer time (not “will decide later”)

- **Work model & location**
  - **Must have**: Hybrid policy written
  - **Acceptable**: **2 days/week office**, location **Kharadi, Pune**; office-day flexibility clarified (fixed vs flexible)

- **Fixed/Base pay (non-negotiable)**
  - **Must have**: fixed/base component called out explicitly in the breakup
  - **Acceptable**: **₹20–21 LPA fixed/base** (minimum to proceed)

- **Variable/bonus**
  - **Must have**: exact %/amount + payout schedule + criteria in writing
  - **Acceptable**: variable is **additional** (not used to dilute fixed below your minimum); payout rules are clear and reasonable

- **Sign-on bonus (preferred lever)**
  - **Must have**: amount + payout timing + clawback terms in writing
  - **Acceptable**: used to bridge any gap vs your expectation (preferred over increasing variable); paid on joining/first payroll; clawback terms clearly defined

- **CTC breakup / in-hand clarity**
  - **Must have**: full breakup (fixed vs variable, allowances, employer PF/gratuity/insurance)
  - **Acceptable**: no hidden components; you can compute expected monthly in-hand confidently

- **Joining date & notice period**
  - **Must have**: expected joining window + notice period policy + buyout/early release info
  - **Acceptable**: joining date aligns with your notice period; flexibility documented if release date shifts

- **Probation / confirmation**
  - **Must have**: probation duration and confirmation criteria (if any)
  - **Acceptable**: terms are standard/transparent; no ambiguous “at discretion” clauses without clarity

- **Leaves (PTO)**
  - **Must have**: annual leave + sick leave + holiday policy in writing
  - **Acceptable**: policy is clear on accrual, carry-forward, and encashment (if applicable)

- **Health insurance**
  - **Must have**: coverage details (self/spouse/parents), sum insured, waiting period, premium split
  - **Acceptable**: family coverage is available and terms are clearly documented

- **Equipment & reimbursements**
  - **Must have**: laptop provided + any WFH/internet reimbursement policy
  - **Acceptable**: clear ownership/procurement process and reimbursement rules

- **Appraisal / revision cycle**
  - **Must have**: next review cycle timing
  - **Acceptable**: you know when you’ll be eligible for first review/revision

- **Offer validity & timelines**
  - **Must have**: offer validity period and decision deadline in writing
  - **Acceptable**: reasonable time to review (not pressured without documentation)
