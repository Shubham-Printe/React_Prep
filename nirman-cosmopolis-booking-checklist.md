## What To Figure Out Before Booking

## A. Builder credibility

You should speak to at least `3 real owners` from delivered Nirman projects.

Questions to ask:
- Was possession delivered on time?
- Were there hidden charges near possession?
- How did the builder behave after most payments were made?
- Were promised amenities and specs actually delivered?
- Any seepage, lift, parking, paperwork, or quality problems?
- Was defect fixing handled properly?

## B. Legal status of the front plot

You should ask sales for:
- the `sanctioned master layout`
- exact marking of `E tower`
- exact marking of the `902 stack`
- exact identification of the land parcel in front

You need the answer to this:

`Why is that land open today, and can it legally become a building later?`

Possible statuses to clarify:
- part of the same project
- reserved open space
- amenity space
- DP road / reservation
- separately developable parcel

If they are vague, assume the open view is temporary.

## C. MahaRERA and approvals

You should verify:
- project registration details
- latest completion timeline
- commencement certificate
- sanctioned plans
- uploaded approvals
- any revised deadlines or revisions

Do not rely only on brochure timelines or verbal statements.

## D. Agreement for Sale

Before paying more than a token, ask for:
- `draft Agreement for Sale`
- complete cost sheet
- payment schedule

Check the following carefully:
- exact `RERA carpet area`
- total price breakup
- floor rise
- parking charges
- GST
- maintenance deposit
- corpus fund
- meter / utility charges
- legal / documentation charges
- society formation or other one-time charges
- possession date
- grace period
- delay compensation if builder delays
- cancellation and refund terms
- what counts as actual possession
- attached specification sheet for materials and fittings

## E. Negotiation

Do not negotiate only on headline price.

Try to improve:
- base price
- floor-rise waiver
- parking inclusion
- payment schedule
- better final installment structure
- inclusion of premium specifications
- written commitments on promised features
