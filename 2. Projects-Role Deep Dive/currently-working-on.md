"I’ve been working for over three years on a project for a US-based client called Hello Chapter, which handles home renovation services in New York and Miami. We’ve built three main web apps — for clients, contractors, and internal teams — plus a content website.

Initially, the project was in trouble — the outsourced developer couldn’t deliver even after multiple extensions. I was brought in to take ownership and get a working MVP out quickly to retain the client. I led the development of a functional demo within a tight timeline, which helped the company secure the project and continue long-term.

Since then, I’ve been leading the frontend team of three developers. We’ve integrated complex third-party features like Draft.js for dynamic content, Gantt charts for project timelines, live camera monitoring, and Tableau-based interactive analytics. Each of these required multiple POCs and architectural adjustments, and they became core differentiators for the client.

A memorable challenge was debugging a legacy PHP + jQuery codebase where table headers weren’t resizable. After inspecting the library, I discovered the root cause was using <td> instead of <th> in hundreds of tables. Rewriting everything wasn’t feasible, so I proposed a custom patch to the library’s resize method — not the standard approach, but it solved the issue in hours instead of days, saving significant time.

Overall, this project has been pivotal — it taught me ownership, pragmatic problem-solving, and how to balance ideal practices with business realities while leading a team and delivering value consistently."
