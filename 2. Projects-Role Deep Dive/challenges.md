"I’ve been working for over three years on a project for a US-based client called Hello Chapter, which is a home renovation company operating in New York and Miami.

Our team built three interconnected web applications — one for client communication, one internal back-office app, and one for contractors — along with a content website. I currently lead the frontend team of three developers, and I’m responsible for architecture decisions, mentoring, and ensuring delivery quality.

When I joined, the project was in a difficult spot — the initial contractor had failed to deliver, so I was brought in to get a working MVP ready. We managed to deliver a functional demo in a short time, which helped retain the client and allowed us to continue development long-term.

Over time, I’ve led multiple feature implementations that required deep research and integrations — for example, embedding Draft.js for dynamic content editing, Gantt charts for project timelines, live camera feeds from renovation sites, and interactive Tableau-based dashboards. These became some of the most impactful and appreciated features by end-users.

One of the more challenging problems involved debugging a legacy PHP + jQuery codebase where data-table headers weren’t resizing properly. After deep-diving into the library’s resize method, I discovered the issue came from using <td> instead of <th> tags across hundreds of tables. Instead of rewriting the entire codebase, I suggested a pragmatic workaround — updating the library’s method to handle <td> elements. It wasn’t the textbook approach, but it saved days of rework and unblocked delivery almost immediately.

Overall, this project has been a major part of my growth — from handling emergencies as a fresher to now leading the frontend, balancing trade-offs, collaborating across backend, design, and stakeholders, and consistently delivering business value through scalable frontend solutions."
