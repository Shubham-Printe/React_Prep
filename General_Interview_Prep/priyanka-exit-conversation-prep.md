# Conversation prep — Director (Priyanka) after resignation

**Guidelines:** **no** internal drama (PM exit, downsizing, raises, colleague’s exit). **No false employer name** — if you **don’t** want to share where you’re going, **say so plainly** and steer back to **LWD** and **handover**. **Location** (Sopan Baug / west Pune) if she pushes “why leave.”

**Outcomes you want:** (1) **Last working day** — or **“confirmed in writing by [date]”** — (2) **handover** agreed in principle, (3) any **early-release** flexibility.

---

## Part A — Main script (use in order)

### Opening

Thanks for making the time, Priyanka. I wanted to speak with you **directly** about my **resignation** — mainly to **align on last working day** and how you’d like **handover** handled.

*[Pause.]*

### Why you’re leaving (default — use unless she pushes you to stay)

I’ve been here **more than four years**. It feels like the **right time for a professional move** — **new scope** and a **change of context**.

**Stop there** — no “nothing personal about Mac,” no culture lecture.

### If she asks “Why now?” or “Anything we should know?”

The main **practical** piece is **location**: my **parents are in Sopan Baug**, I’ve been **in west Pune for work**, and I’ve been **trying to get to a setup where I can live closer to them**. **This next step** fits that.

### If she pushes company name / where

I’m **not comfortable sharing** the **employer name** while I’m still **here** — I’d like to keep that **private**. What I need from you is **clarity on last working day** and **handover**.

### Handover

I’m **committed to a proper handover** — **written** notes on what I own, **KT** with whoever you want in the loop. I will make sure that I do not leave the team in a bad place.

### The ask — early release + LWD (let **her** propose first)

I have a **joining timeline** with my **next employer** that I need to **honour**. I’d like to understand what **last working day** you can **propose** — whether that’s **a week, two weeks**, or another timeline that works with **hiring + KT** on your side.

If you need to **align internally** first, that’s fine — I’d like a **concrete LWD** (or a **clear range**) from you **when** you have it, and **in writing**, so I can **update** my next employer.

**Do not say up front** that **full 60 days is acceptable** — if you hand her that, she’ll **anchor** there. Let her **offer** first; **only** if she’s **fixed** on 60 and won’t move do you decide whether to **accept**, **counter**, or **escalate** (e.g. buyout / HR policy). You still need **LWD in writing** either way.

### If she asks “When do you need to join?”

They’re expecting me **on or before 18 April**. I need **LWD** from you to **lock** the **exact** joining date with them.

### If she asks you to **reconsider**, **stay**, or makes a **counter-offer**

*Use this fuller line here — not in the default flow:*

I’ve been here **more than four years**. I’ve **learned a lot** and I’m **grateful for the time** — it’s been a **meaningful** part of my career. **Right now** it feels like the **right time for a professional move**. I’ve **thought it through**; **my decision to leave is firm**.

Then:

I **appreciate** you **asking** / the **offer**. I’d still rather we use the time to **agree LWD** and **handover**.

### Close

What would help me most is a **concrete proposal** on **last working day** — and if you need time to **align internally**, **when** can I expect that **in writing**?

### If the tone gets sharp

I’m **not** here to argue. I want to **exit respectfully** and **support the transition**. What **LWD** can you **support**?

## Part B — Extra topics (perimeter defence)

Short answers; **don’t** drag or overshare.

### Process & policy

- **Notice start date** — *“Effective date is **[per resignation / HR]** — happy to align with what’s on file.”*
- **Buyout / pay to shorten notice** — Know your **contract**. *“I’m open to **discussing** if policy allows.”* (Only if true.)
- **Leave balance vs notice** — *“Happy to follow **HR policy**.”*
- **Garden leave / WFH during notice** — *“Whatever **you and HR** prefer, as long as **LWD** is clear.”*

### Relationship & optics

- **Why didn’t you come to me first?** — *“I wanted to **decide properly** first; once I was sure, I **acted**.”*
- **Withdraw resignation?** — *“**No** — I’m **firm**. I’d rather align **LWD** and **handover**.”*
- **Late counter (money / title)** — *“I **appreciate** it; I’m **not** reopening. I need **LWD**.”*
- **Guilt / “we invested in you”** — *“I’m **grateful**. This is still the **right** next step for me.”*
- **Client / stakeholder comms** — *“I’ll follow **your** guidance on **who** says **what** and **when**.”*

### Risky or hostile angles (stay calm, short)

- **IP / laptop / what are you taking?** — *“Only **personal** items; **work** stays with the company. I’ll follow **IT / HR** on **return** and **access**.”*
- **Non-compete / non-solicit** — *“I’ll **honour my contract**.”* (Know if your offer letter has clauses.)
- **Bond / training recovery** — *“I’ll go by **what’s in writing** with **HR**.”*
- **Are others leaving?** — *“I can **only** speak for **myself**.”*

### Logistics

- **Who receives handover?** — *“**Mac** / **whoever you assign** — I’ll **document** and do **sessions**.”*
- **When LWD in writing?** — *“I need it **by [e.g. end of week]** so I can **confirm** with my **next employer**.”*
- **Reference later** — *“I’d **hope** we part on **professional** terms; we can **see** when the time comes.”*

### If you truly don’t know

*“I don’t have that **in front of me** — I’ll **check** with **HR** / my **contract** and **revert**.”*
