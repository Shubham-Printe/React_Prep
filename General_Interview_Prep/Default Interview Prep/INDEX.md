# 🚨 The "Panic Button" Protocol: Default Interview Prep

**Instructions:** This is your mandatory checklist. Before **ANY** interview, you must review these documents to ensure your baseline is solid. This is not for deep learning; it's for **activating** what you already know so you don't freeze on basics.

---

## ✅ Step 1: Coding Warmup (The Hands)
*Goal: Ensure you don't stumble on syntax or basic data manipulation.*

- [ ] **[01. Essential JS Methods](./01-essential-js-methods.md)**
    - **Why:** You will likely be asked to process a list of data.
    - **Check:** Can you write a `reduce` function? Can you sort an array of objects? Can you group items into a Map?
    - **Drill:** Write the "Group Anagrams" solution from scratch on a whiteboard/notepad.

---

## ✅ Step 2: React Core (The Framework)
*Goal: Refresh the mental model of component lifecycles and hooks.*

- [ ] **[02. React Patterns & Hooks (Cheatsheet)](./02-react-patterns.md)** *(Coming Soon)*
    - **Why:** You need to explain *why* `useEffect` runs, or how to pass data without Prop Drilling.
    - **Check:** `useEffect` dependency arrays, Custom Hooks structure, `useMemo` vs `useCallback`.

---

## ✅ Step 3: CSS & Layouts (The Visuals)
*Goal: Avoid looking clueless when asked to center a div or build a 3-column layout.*

- [ ] **[03. CSS Layouts (Flex & Grid)](./03-css-layouts.md)** *(Coming Soon)*
    - **Why:** CSS is often the first "filter" in frontend interviews.
    - **Check:** Centering (Horizontal/Vertical), Flex direction, Grid basics, Box Model.

---

## ✅ Step 4: The "Tell Me About Yourself" (The Human)
*Goal: Have your stories ready so you sound confident, not rambling.*

- [ ] **[04. Behavioral Stories (STAR Method)](./04-behavioral-stories.md)** *(Coming Soon)*
    - **Why:** This is 50% of the interview.
    - **Check:** Your "Introduction", 1 "Challenge" story, 1 "Conflict" story, 1 "Success" story.
