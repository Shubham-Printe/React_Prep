# Behavioral Stories (The Human Side)

**Goal:** Have 3-5 adaptable stories ready so you don't ramble. Use the **STAR** method.

## 1. The STAR Method
Structure every answer like this:

*   **S - Situation:** Set the scene (briefly). "We were migrating a legacy app..."
*   **T - Task:** What was your specific goal? "I needed to reduce build times by 50%..."
*   **A - Action:** What did **YOU** do? (Not "we"). "I analyzed the bundle, implemented code-splitting..."
*   **R - Result:** The outcome. "Build time dropped to 2 mins, dev productivity increased..."

---

## 2. The "Tell Me About Yourself"
*The Pitch (2 minutes max).*
1.  **Present:** "Currently, I'm a Frontend Engineer at [Company], working on [Tech Stack]..."
2.  **Past:** "Before that, I worked at [Prev Company] where I learned [Skill]..."
3.  **Future:** "I'm looking for a role where I can focus more on [Performance/System Design], which is why I'm interested in this role."

---

## 3. Essential Stories to Prepare

### Story A: The "Technical Challenge" (Bug/Feature)
*Prompt: "Tell me about a hard bug you solved."*
*   **Situation:** Critical crash or performance bottleneck.
*   **Action:** How you debugged it (DevTools, logs, hypothesis).
*   **Result:** Fixed it + added tests to prevent regression.

### Story B: The "Conflict" (Disagreement)
*Prompt: "Tell me about a time you disagreed with a PM or peer."*
*   **Situation:** PM wanted a feature that would hurt performance.
*   **Action:** I didn't say "no". I gathered data/metrics to show the impact. Proposed a compromise.
*   **Result:** We went with the alternative solution. (Show you are collaborative, not stubborn).

### Story C: The "Mistake" (Failure)
*Prompt: "Tell me about a time you failed."*
*   **Situation:** Missed a deadline or broke production.
*   **Action:** Owned it immediately. Fixed the issue. Conducted a post-mortem.
*   **Result:** Implemented a new process (e.g., better CI checks) so it never happened again.

---

## 4. Questions for the Interviewer
*Always have these ready. It shows interest.*

1.  "What does a typical day look like for this role?"
2.  "What is the biggest technical challenge the team is facing right now?"
3.  "How does the team balance new features vs. tech debt?"
4.  "What does success look like in the first 90 days?"
