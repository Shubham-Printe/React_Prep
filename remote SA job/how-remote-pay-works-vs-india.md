# How international remote pay usually works (from India)

Compare this to a typical **Indian IT/services CTC** where you already know the drill: gross components, PF (employee + employer), gratuity accrual (paid on exit if eligible), professional tax, income tax TDS, reimbursements, and **~₹1.1L/month in-hand** on **₹19.5 LPA** (your ballpark — exact depends on declarations, regime, HRA, etc.).

---

## 1. You are not comparing “LPA” to “USD salary” directly

| Indian setup | Remote international setup |
|--------------|----------------------------|
| **CTC** in LPA, with PF, gratuity, variable | Often a **single headline number**: **annual base in USD** (sometimes + bonus/equity) |
| Employer runs **Indian payroll**; TDS; PF | May be **no Indian entity**; they use another structure (below) |
| Benefits: insurance, leave policy familiar | May be **global benefits** or **cash-only** + you arrange locally |

A **USD 65k** offer is **not** “₹65k equivalent lifestyle calculator” in one line — **FX, tax, and structure** decide what you keep.

---

## 2. Four common structures (what “the setup” is)

### A) Employer of Record (EOR) — you are an “employee,” usually cleanest

A third party (e.g. Deel, Remote.com, Oyster) **employs you on paper** where the law requires it (often **India**), while the **US/global company** is your “client” for workload.

**Typical feel:**

- You get a **local employment contract** (India).
- **Salary** may be fixed in **USD** but **paid in INR** to your Indian bank (common), or sometimes USD account depending on arrangement.
- **TDS** on salary applies like a normal Indian job (with proper employer).
- **PF / ESI** may apply depending on how the EOR maps comp (many still mirror Indian statutory expectations; details vary by provider and contract).
- **Leaves** and **statutory protections** closer to a normal job than freelancing.

**In-hand:** Closer mentally to your current **salary slip** mental model — but **line items won’t match** your old Indian employer 1:1. Read the **offer letter + EOR FAQ**.

---

### B) Direct Indian subsidiary of the foreign company

Some companies have **Pvt Ltd in India** and put you on **Indian payroll** directly.

**Typical feel:** Very similar to an **Indian product company**: Indian salary structure, PF, TDS, etc. Offer might still be discussed in **USD equivalent** internally but **credited in INR**.

---

### C) Contractor / independent professional — highest variance

Contract says **services**, not **employment**. You might invoice monthly (USD or INR).

**Typical feel:**

- Money hits your account as **business / professional income** (not always “salary”).
- **No employer PF**; **no Indian salary TDS slip** like Form 16 from them — you often **deposit advance tax** and file **ITR** with a CA’s help.
- **GST** question matters: **B2B export of services** to a foreign client can have specific treatment; **wrong assumption is expensive**. This is **CA territory**.
- You may need **professional indemnity**, **health insurance**, **no paid leave** unless contract says so — **gross looks higher**, **net + risk** must be modeled.

**In-hand rule of thumb:** Don’t compare contractor **USD gross** to employee **INR in-hand** without subtracting **tax set-aside + insurance + leave + equipment**.

---

### D) Rare edge cases

Overseas payroll with **no India presence** and you’re not under EOR — less common; compliance gets messy fast. If you see this, **verify with a CA** before signing.

---

## 3. What usually hits your bank (cash flow)

### Indian job (your baseline)

1. Employer calculates **monthly payout**.
2. Deductions: **PF (employee)**, **PT**, **TDS**, loans, etc.
3. **Credited to bank** — your **~₹1.1L/month** pattern.

### EOR / Indian entity remote job

1. Similar: **scheduled payroll**, **INR (usually)** to bank.
2. **TDS** appears; ask for **salary structure** breakdown (fixed, allowances, *if* any).
3. If comp is **pegged to USD**, clarify: **who bears FX risk**? (Fixed INR vs USD spot each month.)

### Contractor

1. You invoice → client pays **gross**.
2. You set aside **money for tax** (and GST if applicable).
3. **No automatic PF**; retirement is **your SIP / PPF / NPS**, etc.

---

## 4. PF, gratuity, insurance — the “hidden” comparison

| Topic | Typical Indian IT CTC | Remote international |
|--------|------------------------|----------------------|
| **PF** | Often 12% employee + 12% employer (on basic) | EOR/subsidiary **may** have PF; **contractor: no** unless you self-contribute |
| **Gratuity** | Accrues if employee + eligibility | Same if **Indian employee**; **contractor: generally no** |
| **Health cover** | Group medical | Global policy, **allowance**, or **nothing** — **read offer** |
| **Notice / PIP** | Indian labor norms / contract | EOR contract terms; contractors: **notice in SOW** |

When comparing offers, add a **₹/year value** for benefits you must buy yourself.

---

## 5. Tax (India) — high level only

- If you are **tax resident in India**, **worldwide income** is generally in scope — **how** it’s taxed (salary vs business) depends on structure.
- **DTAA** (Double Taxation Avoidance) can matter if **tax is withheld abroad** — rare in pure India-based setups but possible in weird cases.
- **Old vs new tax regime**, **HRA**, **home loan**, **80C**, etc. still apply on eligible income — your CA should model **two offers side by side**.

**Practical prep:** Ask employer/EOR:

1. Is income classified as **salary** or **contracting**?
2. **Who deducts TDS**, and under which section?
3. **Form 16** or equivalent?

---

## 6. Questions to ask Lynn / HR (copy-paste checklist)

1. **Engagement type:** EOR, Indian entity employee, or contractor?
2. **Pay currency:** INR to Indian bank, or USD? **Who bears FX?**
3. **Components:** Pure fixed base, or allowances? Sign-on, bonus, equity?
4. **PF / insurance / leaves:** What exactly is included?
5. **Hardware, SaaS, internet:** Reimbursed or stipend?
6. **Notice period, IP, non-compete:** Contract terms (especially contractor).

---

## 7. Sanity check vs your current ₹19.5 LPA / ~₹1.1L in-hand

- Your **in-hand** is the right baseline for **monthly life + savings**.
- A **USD offer** must be converted at **realistic FX**, then reduced by **Indian tax + lost benefits** (if contractor), before you say it’s better than ₹1.1L/month **net of goals** (emergency fund, retirement, family).

Use a spreadsheet: **12 months × expected cash in** − **estimated tax** − **benefits you must buy** − **FX buffer**.
